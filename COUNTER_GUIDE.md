## What is the 108 Chant Counter?

The app includes an interactive counter to help you complete the traditional 108 repetitions of any mantra. This sacred number has deep significance in Vedic tradition.

## Why 108?

In Vedic tradition, 108 is considered a sacred number:
- **108 Upanishads** - Sacred texts
- **108 Names** - Many deities have 108 names
- **108 Beads** - Traditional mala (prayer beads) have 108 beads
- **Cosmic Significance** - Distance from Earth to Sun is 108 times the Sun's diameter
- **Chakras** - 108 energy lines converge to form the heart chakra

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Do I have to chant exactly 108 times?
**A:** 108 is traditional and recommended, but any number of repetitions is beneficial. The counter helps you maintain the sacred tradition.

### Q: Can I chant more than 108?
**A:** Absolutely! Click Reset and do another round. Many practitioners do multiple rounds (216, 324, etc.).

### Q: What if I get interrupted?
**A:** The counter stays at your current number. Resume when you can, or Reset and start fresh.

### Q: Can I use this with physical mala beads?
**A:** Yes! Use both together. The app counter can verify your bead count.

### Q: Does it work offline?
**A:** Yes, after the first load, the counter works without internet.
